# Whispers of the Loom

Description:

Whispers of the Loom is a mobile application for collectors of domestic textile antiquities, historians of pioneer-era spinning crafts, and keepers of folk-art heritage, dedicated to cataloging the manual wooden drop spindles, hand-cranked spinning wheels, and mechanical carding combs that processed raw wool and flax before the Industrial Revolution birthed automated steam-powered textile mills.
Systematize your collection of the quiet tools of the hearth—from carved hardwood distaffs and high-wheel walking spinners to iron-toothed wool carding paddles, from mechanical skein-winder clocks that clicked at exact yardages to delicate bone lace-making bobbins from forgotten cottage industries.
Create a digital archive of heavy industry by recording the instrument type, manufacturer, temperature range, era, and the specific steel mill, foundry, or ceramic kiln each specimen once calibrated.

## Bespoke Object Fields (Model "Hearth Fiber Tool"):

•	Spindle Registry Scroll: A unique, system-generated alphanumeric textile catalog code (e.g., WOL-FIBER-4421-WOOL-S).

•	Craft Classification: The specific tier of fiber preparation or spinning architecture (e.g., Walking Wheel, Drop Spindle, Carding Paddle, Skein Winder, Lace Bobbin).

•	Artisan Hallmark: Fictionalized colonial woodworkers, wheelwrights, and folk crafters (e.g., HearthSide Woodworks, Old Colony Wheelwrights, Vanguard Loom & Timber).

•	Orifice & Whorl Geometry: The mechanical dimensions governing the spin dynamics (e.g., 3:1 pulley ratio, weighted soapstone whorl, high-clearance flyer orifice).

•	Timber & Joinery Composition: The exact types of wood and structural joinery used to frame the wheel or spindle (e.g., Mortise-and-tenon seasoned white oak, turned black walnut, aromatic cedar detailing).

•	Teeth Count & Density: For carding tools, the precise layout of the metal combing teeth (e.g., 72 teeth-per-square-inch, fine flax wire array).

•	Physical Proportions: The absolute diameter of the drive wheel or length of the distaff shaft, along with the total weight in grams.

•	Mechanical Soundness: The functional condition of the spinning alignment (e.g., Wheel wobble offset, drive-band groove wear, bearing friction level).

•	Homestead Ground Zero: The fictionalized regional valley, historic settlement, or textile trade route where the tool was actively used (e.g., Shenandoah Valley settlement farm, historic maritime weaving village, forgotten frontier trading post).
